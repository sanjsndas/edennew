Gardening Tips

1. Understand Your Soil: Take the time to test and understand your soil type. It influences what plants will thrive and what amendments may be necessary to improve soil health.

2. Water Wisely: Water your plants early in the morning or late afternoon to reduce evaporation. Use drip irrigation or soaker hoses to target roots and conserve water.

3. Mulch for Health: Applying mulch helps retain moisture, reduce weeds, and regulate soil temperature. Choose organic mulches like bark or compost for added nutrients.

4. Choose Native Plants: Native plants are naturally adapted to your regionâs climate and soil, requiring less water and care while supporting local wildlife.

5. Prune Correctly: Pruning enhances plant health and appearance. Use clean, sharp tools and prune at the right time for each plant species.

6. Protect with Natural Pest Control: Encourage beneficial insects and use organic pest control measures to manage pests without harmful chemicals.

7. Fertilize Thoughtfully: Use slow-release, organic fertilizers to nourish your plants and improve soil structure sustainably.
